Making Brisket

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole beef brisket (10-12 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons kosher salt
  • 2 tablespoons freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Wood chips or chunks for smoking (such as hickory or oak)

How to make it!

  1. Preparing the Brisket:
    • Trim any excess fat from the brisket, leaving a thin layer (about 1/4 inch) on top to provide flavor and moisture during cooking.
    • In a small bowl, combine the salt, black pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, and cayenne pepper (if using).
    • Rub the spice mixture all over the brisket, ensuring it is evenly coated. Wrap the brisket tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.
  2. Preparing the Smoker:
    • Soak your wood chips or chunks in water for at least 30 minutes before smoking. This will help them produce smoke and flavor.
    • Preheat your smoker to a temperature of 225-250째F (107-121째C) according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  3. Smoking the Brisket:
    • Once the smoker is ready, place the brisket directly on the grates, fat-side up.
    • Add the soaked wood chips or chunks to the smoker box or charcoal for continuous smoke production.
    • Close the smoker and maintain a consistent temperature throughout the cooking process. Check the smoker periodically to ensure the temperature remains steady.
  4. Cooking the Brisket:
    • Plan for a cooking time of about 1.5 to 2 hours per pound of brisket. However, cooking times can vary, so it's important to monitor the internal temperature of the meat for doneness.
    • Insert a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the brisket, being careful not to touch bone or fat. The brisket is done when the internal temperature reaches around 195-205째F (90-96째C), and the meat is tender when probed with a fork or meat thermometer.
  5. Resting and Slicing:
    • Once the brisket reaches the desired temperature, remove it from the smoker and loosely tent it with foil. Allow it to rest for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour. This resting period helps the meat retain its juices and makes it easier to slice.
    • After resting, carefully slice the brisket against the grain into thin slices. Serve immediately and enjoy the flavorful, smoky brisket!

Enjoy your smoked brisket!
